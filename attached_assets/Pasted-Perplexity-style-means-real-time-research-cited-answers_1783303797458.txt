Perplexity-style means: real-time research, cited answers, deep research, uploaded-document review, follow-up threads, and an assistant-like workflow, similar to Perplexity/Comet’s AI assistant and research experience.   Federal court forms should include AO 239/AO 240 fee-waiver forms, AO 440 summons, JS 44 civil cover sheet, and Pro Se civil complaint/answer forms.  

Build a full-stack Replit web app called “Pro Se Litigation Navigator.”

Purpose:
Create an AI-powered litigation assistant for ordinary people representing themselves in civil lawsuits. The app must help users understand, organize, draft, and manage pro se litigation, whether they are suing someone or defending themselves.

Important legal positioning:
The app is not a law firm and does not replace an attorney. It provides legal information, drafting assistance, procedural checklists, research support, court-form preparation, and document organization. It must include disclaimers and always encourage users to verify court rules, deadlines, local rules, and legal strategy before filing.

Core concept:
The app should feel like Perplexity-style legal research plus a guided litigation dashboard. It should not look like a basic chatbot. It should feel like a mission-control center for a pro se case.

Build front end and back end.

Use full creative control on the front end, but make it simple, powerful, clean, and beginner-friendly.

Main user paths:

“I want to sue someone”
“I am being sued”
“I received a court document”
“I need help researching case law”
“I need help drafting a filing”
“I need help understanding what happens next”
